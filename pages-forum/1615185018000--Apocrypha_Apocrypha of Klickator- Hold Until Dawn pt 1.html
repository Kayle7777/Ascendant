Hold Until Dawn Part 1

Location: Scylla - Imperial Auxilliary Outpost

Date: 897.M30

Corporal Ronnel Fendt stared out into the desert that surrounded the Imperial Militia supply base that had been his home for the last eleven standard months and let out yet another sigh.

This pathetic outpost that wasn’t even large enough to receive a proper name had to be the most boring posting in the entire nascent Imperium of Man. Ronnel knew he would go insane, but there weren’t many other options for a young man in his position. When the Lunar Templars arrived a generation earlier, Scylla had resisted the Imperial Truth and fought “to the last man” as the former dictator of the world had declared. The Astartes had put the man in chains, and hundreds of thousands of soldiers in the Scyllian Army met their bloody ends at the ends of a bolter.

Soldiers like Ronnel’s parents, for instance. They had been pressed into service in the last, desperate days of the fighting and they had never come home. With no other family, Ronnel found himself an orphan in the care of the new Imperial government. When he reached the age of adulthood, his options were working in the numerous fields on the Agri-World, working in one of the hives that processed the food those fields produced, or working in the Imperial Militia to guard those hives and fields. For Ronnel, it wasn’t even a choice. At least in the Milita, he wouldn’t go mad from the boredom.

Or so he thought. Now that he was on assignment, this was probably the worst of the three options. At least growing or processing the food would allow him to see something that let Ronnel know that his actions had meaning. All he did every day was stand at attention for his pompous commanding officer and watch the shuttles rocket up into space before going to sleep and doing the same thing the next day.

Shuttles like the one that he saw at that very moment, streaking across the sky much faster than he normally saw them travel. Probably some lord on one of the stations in orbit had demanded a luxury, vat grown meal and needed a particular condiment or topping delivered without delay. Typical... only a couple of hours before sundown, but they just had to have their special delivery.

As the shuttle soared overhead, a missile from the nearest hive exploded upon collision with the craft, sending it into a tailspin that resulted in a thunderous crash a minute later. Clouds of smoke, dust, and fire rose up into the air, making for a display of light and sound like nothing that Ronnel had ever heard before.

Emergency sirens that had never been used outside of irregular drills blared out into the dusky sky. The little outpost suddenly became a hive of activity as every single person who had an ounce of authority started barking orders at anyone who would listen.

Ronnel knew this was his chance, his one and only shot at a little bit of excitement. All he had to do was sneak aboard one of these convoy vehicles that was getting ready to go and investigate the action. Who cared if he wasn’t supposed to be on convoy duty. It’s not like anyone would notice.

“Get back to your post, corporal!” a Sergeant Attacord roared as he passed by, grabbing Ronnel by the back of his uniform and shoving him aside. “We’re checking for survivors. Hold this position until we return!”

How very fortunate for him. The first action this world had seen in a generation and it was going to pass Ronnel by. He’d sit back at camp listening to vox chatter and think about how this was going to be the best story he could tell when he was old and spinning tall tales at a bar. Before he could even offer up a protest, the sergeant was in a vehicle that was currently speeding off in the direction of the smoke coming from the wreck. Ronnel was well and truly stuck here now, missing out on yet another adventure.

Grumbling to himself, Ronnel went to work cleaning his lasrifle and taking quick tours around the camp. Though there were technically soldiers who outranked him, they were all specialists in some field or another and as long as Sergeant Attacord was away, he was officially the highest ranking person at this outpost. Well, besides Lieutenant Kisling. But that fool was from some offworld academy and was sent here because he almost washed out of whatever academy spit him out. Ronnel hadn’t seen the lieutenant outside of the mess hall in weeks, and he didn’t think this occasion would be any different.

Hours passed like this, until the low roar of a land raider filled the air. The soldiers were returning, but from the sounds of it, they wouldn’t be coming alone.

Softly at first, drowned out by the roar of the land raider’s engine initially, screams of pain and pleasure filled the night air. Above the din of either the engine or the screams was the unmistakable *crack* of bolter fire.

“Battle positions!” Ronnel roared, as he scrambled to his feet. He was cursing his stupidity. He hadn’t been by to check in on the techpriest in the communications hub. They should have warned him of this by now.

“Priest, why was I not notified of our forces returning?” he asked as he barreled his way towards the comm center. “What is the meaning of thi-”

His words caught in his throat as he rounded the corner and saw what was left of the techpriest. The thing had taken its plasma pistol and fired it into its head until the gun overheated. It had then tried to shove the molten hot barrel into what was left of its face, but the prior damage had already done too much. It lay there smoking, the last vestiges of life sputtering out of it in a series of beeps and gurgles.

Ronnel didn’t even bother to investigate. He just ran towards the front lines. He’d seen death and misery before, growing up on this world it was hard not to. What really terrified him is that he didn’t know there was something that could drive a creature that was half machine to the point of madness.

The lascannons were already starting to fire as Ronnel made his way to the front of the camp. A land raider, followed by the convoy vehicles his sergeant had sent out, was just arriving into camp, followed by literally thousands of screaming people.

They were all in various states of distress, but they all had blood running out of their eyes, ears, nose, and mouth. Still they came, and the screams never stopped.

“Push them back!” Ronnel roared, bringing his lasrifle to bear and unloading onto the charging mass. “Hold the line! Hold the line!”

Miraculously, they held. As quickly as they appeared, the horde left, retreating for reasons only known to them. Hundreds of dead littered the surrounding area, but none of them were from the Militia soldiers. That was a small miracle in and of itself.

Cheers sprung up from the camp, but Ronnel didn’t care. He needed to find the sergeant and figure out what was going on. Normally Ronnel couldn’t stand the man, but his leadership was going to be sorely needed right now. He had a sinking feeling in his gut that this wasn’t the last they had heard of the screamers.

“Sergeant Attacord!” Ronnel bellowed, desperately trying to find him. “Sergeant, where are you?”

As he yelled, hulking figures in grey armor opened the hatch of the Land Raider and began to pile out. They were monstrous, easily taller than the largest man Ronnel had ever seen. He’d heard stories about people like this before. He’d become quite familiar with the tales of these mountainous men.

They were Astartes. Hulking, monstrous, and yet possessed with surprising grace. Ronnel could only remember seeing them once or twice in his life, when he was very young. But seeing an Astartes was not something that a person forgot.

As Ronnel’s squad approached them, he could see that they were in the process of moving bodies that did not survive the chase back to the camp. Most were dressed in a drab grey suit, but there was one that had the size and stature of a Space Marine, as well as a man dressed in a fine outfit that immediately told Ronnel he wasn’t to be messed with. Well, he used to be. His body was horribly burnt and his clothes only had a passing resemblance to their former glory.

His body wasn’t even what they were focusing on. The remaining four Astartes were piling up the corpses haphazardly, but they were paying very special attention to some sort of figure wrapped up in a tarp that was covered in glowing symbols.

“Stop!” one of them bellowed, leveling his bolt pistol at the approaching soldiers as his power sword crackled to life. “One step further and I’ll kill you all!”

Ronnel raised his hands up slowly, still advancing towards the towering figure as he spoke.

“I’m Corporal Ronnel Fendt of Outpost 501.” he stated, desperately trying to sound confident and self assured as he hoped he looked. “We saw your shuttle go down, and we’re here to help.”

“Ave Imperator!” he continued, pleased by his sudden flash of wisdom as he tried to convince this righteous servant of the Emperor to not strike him down where he stood.

“Enough, Belath. They came to help!” an Astartes who was carrying the finely dressed body called out. “They are here to assist, and we need as much of that as we can, brother.”

A noise that could only be described as a resentful sigh escaped from Belath’s helmet. The bolt pistol was quickly holstered as he leaned forward and grabbed Attacord by the front of his uniform.

“Touch anything you’re not supposed to, and I will put a round through your head.” he grumbled as he turned away and went back to securing the area.

“Apologies for my brother.” the second Astartes said, extending his hand. “The Sword is always the most combative and aggressive of us, and for good reason. I am Dannos, the Shield.”

Ronnel had no clue what any of that meant, but he didn’t care. Fortunately, he found Attacord a moment later and motioned for him to come over and help him in talking with these Astartes before they blew his head off because he saluted too quickly.

The sergeant was his usual arrogant self and kept on talking about how much of an honor it was to be in the presence of one of the Emperor’s Angels and all such similar nonsense. The warriors paid him no mind, as they went about their business securing the convoy and tasking soldiers with helping them transport the cargo from the vehicles to the camp.

Oddly enough, each of the soldiers assisting them with inventory operations had to stand before Dannos, who would cup their face in his hands for a brief moment before declaring them ‘pure’ and moving on to the next one. It didn’t make any sense to Ronnel, but he figured that he wasn’t paid nearly enough to try and make sense out of all of this.

“First taste of action, eh Fendt?” Attacord guffawed, slapping Ronnel hard enough that the Corporal stumbled forward a little before regaining his balance.

“Yeah.” Ronnel said, trying not to make eye contact and just keeping his eyes fixed on his boots as he worked. Attacord was by no means a bad sergeant, but he loved to hear himself talk, and the soldiers at Outpost 501 had figured out long ago it was a good idea to keep your head looking at the dirt and he would eventually move on.

“Shame you didn’t ride out with us.” Attacord continued, oblivious to the Corporals discomfort as they passed by the oddly wrapped statue. “That wreck was like nothing else I’d ever seen, and the people who came after us? Strange stuff that. They seemed to be after this thing here, wonder what in the entire galaxy with worth this much troub-”

“Ohhhhhhhh.”

A guttural moan came from Ronnel’s right, but it did not sound like the cry of a wounded man. It was a sound of pure, unadulterated pleasure.

Before Ronnel could act, he felt something collide with him and he saw stars as his head smack violently off the dirt. Blinding pain coursed through him, and white spots blotted out his vision almost entirely.

“She’s beautiful, Fendt.” a voice said, crazed out of its mind to where Ronnel almost could make out the speaker.

“Attacord?”

The sergeant was wrestling with him now, pinning him down and rotating him towards a direction, though Ronnel couldn’t make out which. His eyes were shut tight with pain, and the ringing in his ears made it very hard for him to hear.

“I was blind, but now I see. You’ll see too… she is everything. She is my queen!”

Attacord had his head in a vice-like grip. Maddened fingers scrambled trying to find purchase on his eyelids, but Ronnel buried his head in the ground, unable to do more than wiggle like a worm, but he hoped it would be enough.

A thunderous crack erupted right above Ronnel’s head, and he felt something wet plop onto his head.

“I warned you I’d shoot if you were looking where you weren’t supposed to.” came the rumbling tone of Belath. Just when Ronnel was about to thank his savior, he was hauled up and a power sword was soon at his throat.

“Did you look?” Belath roared.

Ronnel was too scared to answer. He just shook his head vigorously. The energy leaking off the sword made his skin tingle, and he was hoping with all his might that this wasn’t going to be the end of him.

“Hmph.” the giant said, sheathing his sword. “You are made of sterner stuff than most on this planet. Perhaps you won’t die after all.”

Ronnel finally let out the breath that he didn’t even know he was holding. Tears started to well up in his eyes and a pit formed in his stomach as he saw what was left of Attacord’s head spread out all over the sand.

“What is your name, soldier?” Belath asked.

“R-Ronnel Fendt, Corporal.” Ronnel squeaked.

“Well then Corporal Fendt, I think it’s time we met your commanding officer.” Belath said, brushing Ronnel aside as he made his way towards the camp. “It’s time we informed them of what’s going on here, and what’s at stake if we don’t finish our mission.”

Not knowing what else to do, Ronnel just tagged along, following Belath like a dog following its master’s bidding. Just a few short hours ago, Ronnel had been hoping for more action, for a chance to do something memorable that he could think fondly of for decades to come.

There was an old legend on Scylla. Thousands of years ago, a great witch had ruled this entire planet, bewitching her enemies and torturing her subjects before finally being overthrown. Still, even as they burned her body, she diffused her essence into the very planet itself. Wishes had a funny way of coming true on this planet, but never the way that someone wanted. The Imperium had investigated the rumors, but had found nothing to them. Wishes were fanciful, and just a product of an imagination. That was the official Imperial stance at least, most of the locals knew better.

Yet here he was, wishing for action and a story to remember. Ronnel chided himself for his stupidity. If he kept this up there was no way he was going to last the night.

And something told Ronnel Fendt that the night wasn’t done with the soldiers of Outpost 501 quite yet.
